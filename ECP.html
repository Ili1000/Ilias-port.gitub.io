Electrochemical Cell Project

During my chemistry classes, my teacher introduced a graded project based on a concept called problem-based learning. This approach allowed us to choose our own topic and explore it independently, without strict guidelines or limitations from the teacher. I was particularly fascinated by redox reactions and the idea that electrical energy can be generated through chemical processes. This curiosity led me to choose this field as the focus of my project.

My experiment was guided by the question: “How can we generate electricity from everyday food?” To investigate this, we conducted experiments using cola and pickles as potential electrolytes, and analyzed the underlying chemical processes responsible for the observed electrical output. This project started on the first of June 2025 and ended on the third of July 2025.
